Definitions:

  • The Gemma website ("Gemma") encompasses the Gemma web site, underlying database, and software hosted at the University of British Columbia. Obviously if you are using Gemma installed somewhere else or in a private installation, none of this applies!
  • The developers and maintainers of Gemma ("We") are the parties involved in running Gemma's software and hardware.
  • "Use of Gemma" means accessing the Gemma through a web browser, web service or other electronic means, including uploading or downloading data, searching, using analytical tools or visualizations.
  • "Data" or "Expression data" refers to gene expression profiling data of the sort that is stored in Gemma; that is, including but not necessarily limited to microarray data. This is the type of data you can upload!
  • The user of Gemma ("You") are the uploader of data or user of the web site.
  • "Public" data are data which are open to be accessed, analyzed and viewed in Gemma by any user of the web site.
  • "Private" data are data which are limited in access to a subset of users, whether you, another user(s), or the administrators of Gemma.

The rules:

  1. You must be a registered user of Gemma to upload data.
  2. You promise not to upload data which would allow individual identification of humans (in the sense defined by the US HIPAA law). For example, data with the names of human subjects in the sample names is unacceptable.
  3. We will never use non-public data stored in Gemma in any data analysis not explicitly authorized by you, nor will we transmit your data to any third party. We claim no rights over your data other than control over its presence in the Gemma database. The Gemma administrators will have access to your data for the purpose of maintenance only. We never distribute personal information such as email addresses to third parties for any purpose.
  4. While we make every effort to keep your data accurate (as you uploaded it), secure and private (if you request that you data be kept private), uploads and all other uses of Gemma are at your own risk. Neither the University of British Columbia nor us will be held liable for any real or perceived consequences of the use of Gemma. We encourage users with data they consider too sensitive to put on the public Gemma site to consider a local installation of Gemma.
  5. Data are stored in Gemma indefinitely unless you ask us to remove it, though we reserve the right to delete data at any time.
  6. Data that has already been published cannot be stored in Gemma without making the data public. If we discover that you have uploaded published data and have not check the 'make public' box, we reserve the right to make the data public without notifying you. We define 'published' as any dissemination of a description of the expression study in a peer-reviewed publication, even if the expression data themselves were not originally part of the dissemination or supplementary materials. That is, description of the data collection and presentation of a "hit list" constitutes publication of the data. In the case of grievances about public access of data in Gemma, we will be happy to delete your data from the system. The reason for this policy is to encourage the open sharing of data after publication, while allowing Gemma to be used to analyze data prior to publication.
  7. If you use results from Gemma in a publication or scientific presentation, we ask that you cite the web site (see information elsewhere on the site).
